A loan story

Here's how the helpers work together when somebody asks to borrow money.

Notice the bold names — every step is started by a caller (a front-end, a robot, or an external system), never by one helper calling another.

  1. 🦈 A grown-up wants to borrow money. The broker's own system calls Bessie with the details.
  2. 🦈➡️📋 Bessie's response tells the broker's system what to do next — so it then calls Anna, who tucks the new application into a folder.
  3. ◻️ Behind the scenes, the Whitebox Run robot calls Whitney the Wise to do all the magic checks — credit, bank, ID, the lot.
  4. 🕵️ An underwriting robot also calls Sherlock for a peek on Facebook and LinkedIn.
  5. 💬 When everything passes, the Message Factory robot calls Connie to send the customer a friendly "you're approved!" message.
  6. 📋➡️📖 When agents are ready, the Central CRM calls Larry to make a brand-new loan, then calls Anna to mark the application paid out.
  7. 📖➡️💳 Every month, the Payments Factory robot calls Penny to take the payment. The Card Reconcile robot then calls Larry with the result.
  8. 💬 Along the way, Message Factory keeps calling Connie with reminders and statements; Robot Responders reads every reply.
  9. 🔒 When the customer opens the TL App, the app calls Gary first, every time, to check the wristband before anything else.
  10. 🎉 When the loan is paid off — every helper has done its job perfectly, and never once spoken to another helper directly!

How they talk to each other

A few simple ground rules every helper follows.

🎫

Special badges

Helpers only talk to each other if they show a real, valid badge — no sneaking allowed!

🕒

Two clocks

Every helper writes things down twice — once in their own time zone, once in "world time" (UTC).

📒

Nothing thrown away

Every important thing that happens gets a forever-note. You can't change old notes — you just add new ones.

🔔

Alarm bells

If something goes wrong, every helper has their own alarm bell so the team knows to come help.

📥

Big jobs in queues

When there's lots to do, helpers line work up like a queue at the ice cream shop and tackle it one at a time.

🔁

Try, try again

If a job fails, helpers try again — and again — until it works. They never give up.

The Commandments

Every helper had to promise these twelve things before they were allowed to join the team. They're the rules that keep our world tidy — easy to add to, hard to break. Disobeying them is how technical debt is born.
